§ 02 Weekly Analysis
This week saw inbound voice volume drop 50% week-over-week while outbound surged 22% — SL improved marginally to 48.4% but remains far below the 80% target, chat SL remains critically low across all PODs, Member Services callback wait climbed to 16h 43m, and 23 of 25 agents carry at least one Review flag.

Voice inbound dropped to 268 answered (from 535 prior week) while outbound rose to 996 (from 816). Answer rate held at 50.9% despite significantly lower volume — the same underlying capacity constraints are limiting inbound accessibility. Abandon rate of 14.5% (76 calls) is consistent with prior weeks. Service Level improved slightly to 48.4% but no individual queue reached the 80% target. AHT increased to 9m 05s from 8m 23s the prior week, adding pressure to queue capacity.

Chat volume declined to 177 offered (from 209) with 90.4% answer rate. All four PODs continue to miss the 80% SL target — best performer POD4 reached only 36.7%. This consistent multi-week failure to meet chat SL indicates a structural staffing or routing issue in the chat queue that is not self-correcting. No POD exceeded 37% SL this week.

Member Services callback avg wait reached 16h 43m — its worst level in recent reporting. Of 26 callbacks offered to Member Services, 29 were completed (including carryover), but the extreme wait time signals severe queue depth. This queue continues to post the weakest voice answer rate at 51.3% — down from the prior week's 36.2%, a minor improvement driven by lower offered volume.
